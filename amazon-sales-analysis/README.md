This dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of the Amazon Prime Video library, exploring the distribution of over 9,000 titles. It tracks content evolution from 1920 to 2021, offering insights into genre diversity, maturity ratings, and global content production.

📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Total Library Size: 9,687 Titles.

Creative Scope: 5,771 unique Directors across 519 Genres.

Longevity: Spans over a century of content (1920 – 2021).

Maturity Diversity: 25 different rating classifications.

🔍 Visualizations & Findings
1. Content Type Split (Movies vs. TV Shows)
The library is heavily weighted toward feature films.

Movies: 7,810 titles (80.82%)

TV Shows: 1,850 titles (19.18%)

2. Rating Distribution
The platform caters significantly to older teenagers and adults.

Top Rating: 13+ (over 2,100 titles).

Significant Segments: 16+ (1.5K) and 18+ (1.2K).

Family Friendly: Titles rated ALL account for roughly 1.3K entries.

3. Geographical Distribution
The map visualization shows a high concentration of content originating from:

North America (United States and Canada)

India

Europe (UK and France)

Brazil

4. Growth Trends (Release Year)
A historical look at the library shows exponential growth starting in the late 1990s, with a massive surge in content production and acquisition between 2010 and 2021.

🛠️ Technical Stack
Tool: Power BI

Mapping: Microsoft Bing Maps Integration

Data Source: Amazon Prime Video Dataset (Titles, Ratings, Directors, and Countries)

🚀 Key Takeaways
Platform Strategy: Prime Video functions primarily as a movie-centric platform compared to some competitors who focus more on episodic TV content.

Audience Targeting: The content leans toward the 13+ to 18+ demographic, suggesting a strategy focused on young adults and mature audiences.

Modern Expansion: The vast majority of the library consists of "modern era" content released in the last decade.
